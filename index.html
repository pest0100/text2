I really wanted to know how to write and be able to express my feelings without sounding like a child. I'm not good with wordplay or anything like that; my words almost never follow spelling rules (even more so if I write in another language). I lose sight of the main point of what I write or try to write, I get lost in the ideas, and I almost always forget the proper punctuation and remember that I'm not cultured enough to write poems and that it would be a shame if I submitted one. But I never forget the feeling, the butterflies that flutter in my stomach for hours, the nights thinking about us, the places I visited and thought, "He would really like this place," the times I felt inspired by you and tried to do the same and saw myself fail many times. Ignore my poor vocabulary and notice my heart rich in love. Ignore if I make spelling mistakes; my intention will always be the same if I start getting it right. To love you. I don't know how we still talk about other people when the person I truly want is you, and leaving you was one of the hardest things I've ever done. I remember it like it was yesterday when we were in the park, it was a Tuesday (December 2nd), and we had a conversation that made me think a lot about how important you are to me and how I managed to go so long without knowing you before. Forever grateful for having met you.
